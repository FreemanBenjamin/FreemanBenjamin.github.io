Headphone Holder

To save space on my desk, I designed and printed this headphone holder. The design is fairly simple - I essentially modeled it after a wall-mounted hose hanger. To start, I sketched the profile of the rounded portion in 2D. As can be seen in the drawing, this consisted of a solid back wall, the surface for the headphones to sit on top of, and raised edges on either side to prevent slipping. Then, using this sketch, I revolved it 180 degrees to create the arched portion. To make the holder adaptable to different sizes of desks, I then created two ledges spaced apart from each other - one to place on top of the desk, and another with a hole for a screw that could be used to tighten the holder against the lip of the desk. Using Autodesk's parts library, I made a hole for an M10 screw and imported a pre-built M10 hex-head screw for the assembly. Then, I plated the screw and holder files and generated the stl files to print. Unfortunately, as can be seen in the images below, I neglected to add supports to the first print. Surprisingly, the arched portion printed successfully until the very top of the arch. However, the upper ledge failed for the first few layers and left a large quantity of unbonded filament. I attempted to flatten the layers with a heat gun and clean metal scraper, but I was not successful.

For the second print, I decreased the size of the raised edges on either side of the arch and generated supports for areas where overhang was greater than 80%. The supports can be seen in the two images below.

After removing the supports, I used a tap and die set to thread the hole and screw using M10 x 1.5 threading.

The assembled headphone holder can be seen in the following images. The only change that I would potentially make to the design would be to decrease the total width of the holder, but it easily supports my current headphones, fits snugly against the desk, and feels quite sturdy.
