Hypervelocity Impact Resistant Nanocomposites

Abstract

Spacecraft in orbit around Earth are routinely subjected to the threat of hypervelocity impact (HVI) by micrometeoroids and orbital debris. An HVI is highly energetic and can cause catastrophic damage to space structures. Therefore, it is imperative that new materials developed for use in space structures are tested for their HVI response. In this project, we developed a composite system with ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene fabric in an epoxy matrix and reinforced with nickel- coated single walled carbon nanotube 'buckypaper'. The HVI response of this composite was tested using a two-stage light gas gun and compared against pristine 'buckypaper' composites. Images of impact were captured using an ultra-high speed camera. Post-test analysis included evaluation of the debris cloud characteristics and the secondary plate impacts.
